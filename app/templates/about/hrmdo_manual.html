I. Legal Basis

This Manual is adopted pursuant to:

  • Republic Act No. 7160 (Local Government Code of 1991)
  • Civil Service Law, rules, and issuances of the Civil Service Commission (CSC)
  • Other relevant laws, rules, and administrative orders governing public service

II. Declaration of Policy

The Local Government Unit shall promote a professional, responsive, and merit-based human resource system anchored on:

  • Merit and Fitness
  • Equal Opportunity
  • Transparency
  • Accountability
  • Efficiency and Effectiveness

All personnel actions shall be governed by Civil Service laws and applicable regulations.

III. Scope and Coverage

This Manual applies to:

  • All officials and employees of the LGU
  • All plantilla and non-plantilla personnel, unless otherwise specified
  • All human resource transactions and processes

IV. Organizational Roles and Responsibilities

  1. Local Chief Executive (LCE)
    • Exercises authority over all personnel actions: Appointment, Promotion, Discipline, Separation
    • Ensures compliance with laws and CSC rules
  2. Human Resource Management and Development Office (HRMDO)
    • Implements HR policies, systems, and procedures
    • Maintains personnel records (201 files)
    • Facilitates recruitment, training, and performance management
    • Ensures compliance with Civil Service rules
  3. Personnel Selection Board (PSB)
    • Assists in recruitment and promotion
    • Evaluates applicants based on merit and fitness
    • Submits recommendations to the LCE
  4. Department Heads
    • Supervise personnel
    • Evaluate employee performance
    • Recommend training and disciplinary actions

V. Recruitment, Selection, and Appointment Policy

A. General Policy

  • Recruitment shall be based on merit and fitness
  • Equal opportunity shall be ensured for all qualified applicants
  • Nepotism is strictly prohibited

B. Procedures

  1. Vacancy Identification
    • Department Head submits request to HRMDO
    • HRMDO verifies plantilla and budget
  2. Publication of Vacancy
    • HRMDO posts vacancy for 15 days in at least 3 conspicuous places
  3. Application Processing
    • HRMDO receives and screens applications
  4. Evaluation
    • PSB evaluates and ranks candidates
  5. Recommendation
    • PSB submits shortlist to LCE
  6. Appointment
    • LCE issues appointment
  7. CSC Submission
    • HRMDO submits appointment for CSC attestation

VI. Onboarding and Placement Policy

  • All newly hired employees shall undergo orientation
  • Required documents must be submitted prior to assumption
  • Employees shall be assigned duties based on job description

VII. Performance Management Policy

  • Performance shall be evaluated based on established standards (IPCR/OPCR)
  • Evaluation shall be conducted semi-annually
  • Results shall be used for: Promotion, Training, Rewards

VIII. Training and Development Policy

  • The LGU shall implement a continuing capability development program
  • HRMDO shall prepare an annual training plan
  • Training shall be based on identified competency gaps

IX. Leave Administration Policy

  • Leave benefits shall follow Civil Service rules
  • Applications must be filed in advance and approved by authorized officials
  • HRMDO shall maintain leave records

X. Discipline and Administrative Cases

  • All employees shall adhere to the Code of Conduct
  • Administrative cases shall follow due process: Filing of complaint, Notice and response, Investigation, Decision by LCE

XI. Promotion Policy

  • Promotions shall be based on: Merit and fitness, Performance rating, Qualification standards
  • PSB shall evaluate and recommend candidates

XII. Records Management Policy

  • HRMDO shall maintain accurate and updated personnel records
  • Confidentiality of records shall be strictly observed
  • Records include: 201 files, Service records, Leave and performance records

XIII. Separation and Exit Policy

  • Separation may be due to: Resignation, Retirement, Dismissal
  • Clearance is required prior to final separation
  • HRMDO shall process exit documents

XIV. Compliance and Monitoring

  • HRMDO shall ensure compliance with all HR policies
  • Regular audits and reviews shall be conducted
  • Violations shall be subject to administrative sanctions

XV. Final Provisions

  • Amendments may be made as necessary in accordance with laws and CSC issuances
